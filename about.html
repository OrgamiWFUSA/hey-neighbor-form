Our story

We built the village
we wished we had.

Hey Neighbor started with a simple idea — that parents in NYC shouldn't have to feel alone in one of the most connected cities in the world.

How it started

A city full of people.
Nobody knows their neighbors.

When our founder moved to the Upper East Side with a newborn, she knew almost no one. The city felt enormous. The days felt long. And despite being surrounded by millions of people, the loneliness of early parenthood hit harder than expected.

She started showing up to the park at the same time every Saturday. She introduced herself to other parents. She organized a dinner. Then another. Then a few more parents joined. Then a few more after that.

"I didn't set out to build a community. I just needed one. And it turned out, so did everyone else."

Hey Neighbor grew out of that simple instinct — that parents want connection, they just need someone to create the conditions for it. We do that through great events, a warm community, and a belief that showing up for each other is worth it.

Today Hey Neighbor brings together hundreds of families across NYC's neighborhoods. We're just getting started.

What we believe

Our values

🏙️
Neighborhood first
Community works best when it's local. Every Hey Neighbor event is designed around where you actually live — so you meet people you might run into at the coffee shop, the park, or the school gate.
🤝
No performance required
We're not a networking event. We're not a parenting class. We're just a group of parents who like showing up for each other. Come as you are, tired and all.
✨
Worth showing up for
Every event we plan has to pass one test — would we actually want to go? If the answer is yes, we put it on the calendar. We sweat the details so you don't have to.
🌱
Built to grow
We started on the Upper East Side. We're now in three neighborhoods and growing fast. Our goal is to bring this same feeling to every neighborhood in NYC — and eventually beyond.
